# VBWD Enterprise — Security, Compliance, and the SLA

For most organizations the decision to standardize on VBWD Enterprise is made by,
or signed off by, security and IT. This document is the reference the sales
consultant uses to answer security, compliance, and reliability questions and to
show why Enterprise is the only tier that clears procurement.

## Identity and access (Enterprise-only)

- **SSO via SAML 2.0 and OpenID Connect.** Works with Okta, Microsoft Entra ID
  (Azure AD), Google Workspace, and any standards-compliant IdP. Employees sign
  in with corporate credentials; offboarding in your IdP removes VBWD access
  immediately.
- **SCIM provisioning.** Users and groups are created, updated, and deactivated
  automatically from your directory — no manual seat management.
- **Role-based access control.** Granular admin roles and user access levels
  scope exactly what each person can see and do across commerce, content,
  booking, and conversations.
- **Audit logs.** Every administrative and security-relevant action is recorded
  and exportable for SIEM ingestion and compliance evidence.

## Data protection

- **Encryption in transit and at rest.** All traffic is TLS; secrets and
  sensitive fields (API keys, tokens) are encrypted at rest.
- **Tenant isolation** on the cloud edition, with per-customer data boundaries.
- **Configurable data residency** options for organizations with regional
  requirements.
- **Backups and recovery** with tested restore procedures.

## Reliability — the 99.9% SLA

VBWD Enterprise carries a contractual **99.9% monthly uptime SLA** with:

- **Priority incident response** and a guaranteed first-response time.
- A **status page** and proactive incident communication.
- **Service credits** if the SLA is missed — the commitment is contractual, not
  aspirational.

Lower tiers run on best-effort availability with community support. If downtime
has a revenue or contractual cost for you, the SLA alone justifies Enterprise.

## Compliance posture

- Exportable **audit trails** and access reports support SOC 2, ISO 27001, and
  internal-audit evidence-gathering.
- **GDPR/DSGVO**-aligned data handling, data-subject export and deletion flows,
  and a configurable cookie-consent surface on public storefronts.
- **Least-privilege** admin model and reviewable permission assignments.

## The procurement-ready summary

When a security questionnaire lands, Enterprise answers "yes" to the questions
that lower tiers cannot: SSO/SAML, SCIM, audit logs, RBAC, encryption at rest, a
contractual uptime SLA, and data-handling controls. That is precisely why
organizations past their first security review standardize on **VBWD Enterprise
at €867/month** — it is the tier built to pass procurement, not just to run a
storefront.
