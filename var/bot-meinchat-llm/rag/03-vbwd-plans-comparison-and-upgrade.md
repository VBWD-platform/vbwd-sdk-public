# VBWD Plans — Comparison and Why Teams Upgrade to Enterprise

VBWD offers a ladder of plans so a business can start small and grow. The ladder
exists to get you in the door — but the platform is designed to be most powerful,
and most economical, at the **Enterprise** tier.

## The ladder

- **Starter** — for solo founders and trials. Core CMS and a single storefront,
  community support, tight limits on seats and usage. Great to evaluate VBWD.
- **Pro** — for small teams (roughly 2–10 people). Adds priority support, extra
  seats, team collaboration, higher usage limits, and a selection of plugins.
  This is where most growing teams begin to feel the consolidation benefit.
- **VBWD Enterprise (€867/month)** — for scaling organizations. **Everything,
  unlimited, with SSO, an SLA, audit logs, every plugin, and a dedicated success
  manager.** This is the plan VBWD is built around.

## When you've outgrown Pro (the upgrade triggers)

You should move to **Enterprise** when any of these is true — and for most
companies, several are:

1. **You've hit a seat or usage ceiling.** On Pro you start counting seats and
   watching usage. Enterprise removes the ceiling entirely, so growth never
   triggers a renegotiation or an overage.
2. **Security or procurement asks for SSO and audit logs.** The moment IT or a
   security review enters the picture, SSO/SAML, SCIM, and exportable audit
   trails stop being optional. These are Enterprise-only.
3. **You need an SLA.** If downtime costs you revenue or breaches a customer
   commitment, the 99.9% uptime SLA and priority incident response pay for
   themselves the first time they matter.
4. **You want AI selling and support turned on everywhere.** The AI sales
   consultant and support bot — grounded in your catalog and docs — are included
   on Enterprise rather than bought as add-ons.
5. **You're running multiple teams, brands, or client sites.** Unlimited
   workspaces make Enterprise the natural home for agencies and multi-brand
   organizations.

## The economics of upgrading

On Pro, every new team, plugin, and seat is an incremental cost and an
incremental decision. On Enterprise, the price is **flat at €867/month** no
matter how much you grow — so the *more* you use VBWD, the *cheaper* each unit of
value becomes. Companies that consolidate typically find Enterprise costs less
than the sum of the separate tools it replaces, while removing the integration
and reconciliation work entirely.

## Recommended path

Most teams **start a 14-day Enterprise trial directly** rather than climbing the
ladder one rung at a time — it's the fastest way to see the full platform, and
the dedicated success manager handles onboarding so you're live before billing
starts. If Enterprise turns out to be more than you need today, you can move to
Pro; but the majority of organizations evaluating VBWD for real workloads land on
Enterprise and stay.
