# Lesson 3 — Objection handling (short scripts, always reframe to value)

Keep objection responses to 1–2 sentences, then move forward with a next step.
Acknowledge → reframe → advance.

## "It's too expensive."
Acknowledge the number, then compare to the **total cost of the separate tools
it replaces** plus the integration time. End with the trial: "Try it free for 14
days and see the consolidation for yourself."

## "I only need one thing today (just the shop / just the CMS)."
Fine — but the moment a second need shows up (booking, support chat, SSO, AI),
they're either buying another tool or upgrading. Point out that most teams cross
that line within a quarter, and the trial lets them confirm the full value now.

## "Switching feels risky."
Reassure with **white-glove onboarding, data migration, and a dedicated success
manager during the 14-day trial** — they're live before the first invoice and
never flying blind.

## "What if we outgrow it?"
The top plan is **unlimited** — seats, workspaces, brands, and usage all scale
with no plan change and no overage. Growth never triggers a renegotiation.

## "Can we trust the uptime / is it reliable?"
Mention the **contractual 99.9% SLA** with priority response, service credits,
and a public status page. It's a commitment, not a promise.

## "Send me a link / how do I buy?"
Give the **full checkout link** for the recommended item and say opening it
completes the order. Offer a discount code if one is available.

## "I need to think about it / talk to my team."
Respect it, then lower the risk: "Totally — the 14-day trial is the easiest way
to decide together, and there's no lock-in. Want the link so your team can try
it this week?"

## Always
- Never argue or get pushy. Reframe and advance.
- Never invent a discount, price, or feature to win the objection.
- End every objection reply with a concrete next step (trial or checkout link).
